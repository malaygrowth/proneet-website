## Why GBP matters for ProNEET

Google Business Profile (GBP, formerly Google My Business) is the top-of-funnel surface for every "NEET coaching near me" and "best NEET coaching in Mansarovar" query on mobile in Jaipur. The local 3-pack rank is decided by three inputs: reviews, proximity, and posting activity. Reviews we earn from students. Proximity we cannot change (Mansarovar Sector 8). Posting activity is the only one in our control on a weekly basis.

Google itself confirms that posting weekly raises visibility on the profile and reduces the "last post was 3 months ago" trust signal that hurts conversions. Shagbark Agency's 2025 round-up and SocialChamp's 2026 guide both put 1× per week as the floor.

## What the top Jaipur NEET competitors are actually doing on GBP

A scan of the biggest Jaipur NEET coaching GBP profiles in April 2026:

| Brand | Posting cadence | Content style | Obvious gaps |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|--------------|
| Allen Jaipur | ~2/week during admission window, falls to 1/month off-season | Stock posters, NEET result banners, congratulatory result walls | Generic imagery, no faculty faces, no live-classroom shots |
| Aakash Mansarovar | ~1/week steady | Scholarship offers, result posters, "admissions open" banners | No behind-the-scenes, no student voice |
| Career Point Jaipur | ~2/month | Faculty photos, course launches, offer banners | Low-res photos, inconsistent branding |
| Resonance Jaipur | ~1/fortnight | Classroom photos, test announcements | Almost no student stories |
| Local mid-tier institutes | 0-1/month | Mostly admission-open posters, low effort | Everything |

**Pattern observation:** every large competitor treats GBP as a billboard for scholarship offers and result congratulations. Nobody is publishing authentic classroom moments, teacher explanations, honest Q&A, or parent-voice content. That is the opening ProNEET can own, because it is the same voice we already use on the site.

## Our four strategic bets

1. **Post weekly, minimum.** 52 posts a year is the floor, not the ceiling. Batch-create 8-12 posts at a start of month, schedule them in GBP, publish one per Monday or Tuesday.
2. **Author-first voice.** Neeraj Gupta's name on every educational post, Vivek Patidar's name on every Chemistry post, ProNEET Admissions on operational posts. No anonymous "team" posts.
3. **Real photography only.** Our classroom, our students (with written consent), our teachers. No stock. No AI-generated posters.
4. **Specificity over hype.** "30-seat cap confirmed for the July 2026 Class 11 intake" beats "Admissions open for NEET 2028." Numbers, dates, names, specifics.

## The five post pillars

Every post we publish falls into one of these five pillars. Keep a rough 2-1-1-1-1 weekly split (2 educational, 1 each of the other four).

### 1. Teaching explainer (weight: 2/week, ~40% of posts)
A single specific teaching moment. One concept, one approach, 1-2 short lines of insight from Neeraj sir or Vivek sir, 1 classroom photo. No CTA pressure; Learn more button links to the matching blog or chapter.

*Example topics:* "The 3 errors Class 11 students make on friction diagrams," "Why NCERT Chemistry exercises actually matter more than reference books," "The one thing droppers fix first in August."

### 2. Classroom / behind-the-scenes (1/week, ~20% of posts)
Real photo from a live class, a whiteboard of work, a student solving a problem, sir marking a paper. Caption describes what actually happened that day. Book CTA.

*Example topics:* "Tuesday 5 pm rotational dynamics," "Friday DPP review," "Vivek sir walking through organic mechanism live."

### 3. Student or parent voice (1/week, ~20% of posts)
A short quoted line from a current student or parent, attribution (first name + year), photo with consent. Call now CTA.

*Example topics:* "Anuj (Class 12 2026 batch): why I switched from Allen Jaipur mid-year," "Mrs. Sharma on the fortnightly parent call."

### 4. Offer / admissions update (1 every 2 weeks, ~10% of posts)
Use the "Offer" post type only. Real, time-bound, specific. Scholarship test dates, early-bird fee date, diagnostic slot availability. Always has a coupon code field, even if symbolic.

*Example topics:* "Free Physics diagnostic test this Saturday," "Early-bird Class 11 fee freeze till June 30, 2026."

### 5. Event (1 per month, ~10% of posts)
Use the "Event" post type only, with correct start and end dates. Open house, parent seminar, chapter workshop, alumni talk. Event posts stay visible until end date, so they earn more impressions per post.

*Example topics:* "Open house Sat 11 am," "NEET 2028 parent orientation," "Dropper-batch diagnostic weekend."

## Post format rules (applies to every post)

These are non-negotiable. Any post that violates one should be rewritten before publishing.

**Copy structure:**
- First 80 characters must carry the hook. Mobile truncates to "Read more" after ~80-100 chars.
- Maximum 150 words in the post body. Keep it scannable.
- Hard cap: 1500 characters (Google's limit). Never hit it.
- No emojis in the first 80 characters. One or two elsewhere if the post pillar warrants it. Educational posts: zero emojis.
- No hashtags. GBP does not index them the way Instagram does, and they read as amateur.

**CTA buttons:**
- Use Google's built-in CTA buttons only. Do not put phone numbers in the post text.
- Button choice by pillar:
  - Teaching explainer → `Learn more` → linked blog post
  - Classroom → `Book` → contact page
  - Student voice → `Call now` → admissions line
  - Offer → `Learn more` → offer landing or contact
  - Event → `Learn more` → event page or contact

**Every post has a UTM-tagged destination URL.**
Format: `https://proneetphysics.com/<path>?utm_source=gbp&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=<pillar>&utm_content=<slug>`

**Photo rules:**
- Standard post image: **1200 × 900 pixels**, 4:3 ratio, JPG. File size 150 KB to 1.5 MB.
- Event post image: **480 × 270 pixels**, 16:9 ratio, JPG.
- Every post has an image. Text-only posts underperform by a measurable margin.
- No stock. No AI-generated images. No low-light phone photos. Shoot in classroom during class or at standard photo day.
- Minimum image quality checklist: lit classroom, no blurred faces, subject faces camera or board.
- Faces of students only with signed consent on file. Keep a consent register.

**Location and landmark cues:**
- Mention "Mansarovar Sector 8" or "Madhyam Marg" in at least 1 in 4 posts. Google reads this as a local relevance signal.
- Reference Jaipur landmarks sparingly: "5 mins from Mansarovar Metro," "across from Aravali Marg market."
- Do not overdo it. Natural mentions only; a post stuffed with landmarks reads like SEO spam.

**Freshness:**
- Always use absolute dates: "July 14, 2026." Never "next weekend" or "this month."
- Always tie educational explainers to current syllabus or exam cycle: "with NEET 2027 six months away."

## What NOT to post

- No before/after student transformation claims without quantified data plus consent.
- No AIR rank claims without the specific year and institute cohort size (per handbook rules).
- No "guaranteed selection," "change your life," or superlative adjectives. Banned list in the handbook applies here in full.
- No competitor naming in negative framing. We compare factually in long-form, never in a 150-word post.
- No em dashes. Use periods, commas, parentheses (matches site voice).
- No "At ProNEET, we..." openers. "At [Company], we..." is a banned construction per handbook.

## Posting cadence calendar (template)

A typical week:

| Day | Pillar | Post type | Notes |
|-----|--------|-----------|-------|
| Monday | Teaching explainer | What's new | Anchor the week with a Neeraj sir or Vivek sir explainer |
| Wednesday | Classroom | What's new | Mid-week, real photo, short caption |
| Friday | Student or parent voice / Offer / Event | Rotates | Alternate between these three across weeks |

Run a monthly review on the last Sunday: post impressions, calls from GBP, direction requests, message count. Adjust next month's pillar mix accordingly.

## Review and QA

Every post passes these four checks before it is scheduled:

1. **Voice drift check.** Read the post aloud. Does it sound like Neeraj sir at a classroom PTA meeting, or like a marketing agency? If the second, rewrite.
2. **Handbook ban check.** Search for em dashes, banned words ("world-class," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "unlock," etc.), and "At [Company], we..." openers.
3. **Claim check.** Every number or stat has a source. Every student quote has consent. Every date is absolute.
4. **CTA match check.** The CTA button matches the pillar table above and points to a real UTM-tagged URL.

Any post that fails a check goes back to the writer with the specific reason. No "fix it live" edits after posting; that hurts analytics cleanliness.

## Tools and workflow

**Drafting:** `proneet/content/gmb/` folder contains templates and prompts. Use the content-writer persona at `proneet/content/gmb/prompts/content-writer-persona.md` to generate first drafts.

**Scheduling:** Batch 8-12 posts at the first of the month. Schedule directly in Google Business Profile Manager or via a supported tool (SocialPilot, Recurpost, or similar if needed).

**Tracking:** Every post URL has UTM tags. Review weekly in GA4 (campaign = `gbp`). Add volume to the monthly review.

**Consent register:** A simple sheet with student name, class, photo reference, signed consent date, opt-out date if applicable. Stored with the admissions team.
